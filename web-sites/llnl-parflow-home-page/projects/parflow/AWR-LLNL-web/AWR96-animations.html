Animation Sequences

Several animated (mpeg) sequences are available to help the reader visualize various simulations and conceptualizations referred to in the paper. They are meant to complement several of the "still" figures used in the text. Please note the memory requirements, as many of these files are rather large in size.

Hydrostratigraphic Units. Animation sequences 3a and 3b accompany Figure 3 and show more of the structure of the detailed modeling subdomain and hydrostratigraphic units. In these figures, only constant properties are assigned to the layers, as used in the sixth and seventh medium realizations.

  • Sequence 3a shows the domain with constant layer properties and the initial location of the tracer plume (1.6Mb).

  • Sequence 3b shows the domain with constant layer properties and its final location, as found in the sixth flow realization under "ambient-extraction" conditions and local scale dispersion (1.5Mb).

Realization of Heterogeneity. Animation sequences 7a nd 7b accompany Figure 7 and show more structure in the detailed modeling subdomain and the hydrostratigraphic units. In these figures, nonuniform properties are assigned probabilistically to the layers according to distributions inferred from measured data (as in Figures 4, 5, and 6). Here, we show the first of five realizations generated in this fashion.

  • Sequence 7a shows the first heterogeneous medium realization with the initial location of the tracer plume (3.9 Mb).

  • Sequence 7b shows the first heterogeneous realization with the final location of the tracer plume, as found under "ambient-extraction" flow conditions (4.1 Mb).

2-D Montage of Tracer Migration. Animation sequences 10a and 10b accompany Figure 10 and show collectively the evolution of vertically-averaged tracer plumes in the the first six medium realizations.

  • Sequence 10a shows the tracer plumes are shown evolving under "ambient-only" conditions for 90,000 days with no remedial pumping (1.6Mb). Recall that the problem in the sixth realization (upper right) was conducted with constant geometric-mean conductivities in the hydrostratigraphic layers (as in sequences 3a and 3b and Figure 3) with local scale dispersivities.

  • Sequence 10b shows the same tracer plumes are shown evolving under "ambient" conditions for 15,000 days, after which "extraction" conditions were maintained with the TFB well turned on (1.5Mb).

3-D and 2-D Plume Migration Animations. The final animation sequences are meant to accompany both Figure 10 and Figure 11 and show individually the evolution of the three-dimensional and vertically-averaged plumes in different medium realizations under both "ambient-only" and "ambient-extraction" conditions. In addition, separate sequences are available for both the tracer and reqactive compounds, for a total of 20 separate animations.

  • Sequences 11Ta (4.0Mb), 11Tb (3.9Mb), 11Tc (4.2Mb), 11Td (3.4Mb), and 11Te (4.2Mb) show the evolving tracer plumes found under "ambient-only" conditions in the first five heterogeneous medium realizations. Sequence 11Tf (2.5Mb) corresponds to the sixth uniform realization with geometric mean hydraulic conductivities and local scale dispersivities. Each sequence shows a three-dimensional view and the corresponding vertically-averaged view, as used in the montages in sequences 10a and 10b.

  • Sequences 11Tg (3.3Mb), 11Th (3.2Mb), 11Ti (3.5Mb), 11Tj (3.1Mb), 11Tk (3.6Mb), and 11Tl (2.6Mb) show the exact same simulations under "ambient-extraction" conditions. Here, the "TFB" extraction well is turned on after 15,000 days of migration under ambient conditions.

The final 8 animation sequences show the evolution of the sorbing, or reactive, plumes under the same scenarios as above.

  • Sequences 11Ra (3.0Mb), 11Rb (3.0Mb), 11Rc (3.2Mb), 11Rd (2.7Mb), and 11Re (3.2Mb) correspond to reactive plume migration in first five nonuniform medium realizations under "ambient-only" conditions; Sequence 11Rf (2.5Mb) corresponds to reactive migration in the sixth uniform realization.
  • Sequences 11Rg (3.0Mb), 11Rh (2.8Mb), and 11Ri (3.1Mb) correspond similarly to reactive migration behavior in the first three realizations under "ambient-extraction" conditions.
